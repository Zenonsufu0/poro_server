# Greatsword additional rules

- Greatsword silhouettes must be dramatic and readable.
- Do not create a generic rectangular blade.
- The blade must have taper, tip shaping, and at least one distinctive silhouette feature from the reference.
- The guard must have readable structure, not only a straight bar.
- The handle and pommel must be visually separate.
- Complex fantasy greatswords should normally use 30–80 cubes in blockout.
- If the reference has layered blade plates, crescent guards, wing-like guards, or crystal cores, represent them with geometry during blockout.
- Texture-only representation of major silhouette features is not allowed.
- For first blockout, similarity to reference is more important than low cube count.
